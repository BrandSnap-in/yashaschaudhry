Who I am

Builder first.
Student second.

I built PhishSentinel because my grandfather lost his bank credentials to a phishing scam. I built Battery Bin because nobody else had put a recycling bin in the right place. I built Hardware Decoded three times after it got destroyed twice. I built BrandSnap from one favour that turned into a business. The pattern is always the same: problem spotted, solution built, kept running.

🔍

Problems first, tools second

Every project on this site started with a problem I personally witnessed — a grandfather scammed, a landfill overflowing, a community that did not exist. The technology always came second. The problem always came first.

🌱

Impact over presentation

75+ kg of toxic batteries not in a landfill. A grandfather who browses the web with confidence. Seven local businesses with a real digital presence. The numbers that matter most do not appear on any certificate.

🎵

Long-term commitment

I have played flute since Grade 3. I have maintained Hardware Decoded since Grade 8 — rebuilt it twice after raids without stopping. The same patience shows up in every project. Things that last require someone willing to stay with them.

⚡

Bias toward action

Most of what I have built was not assigned, not required, not expected. It just seemed worth doing. I do not wait for the right time or for someone else to prove it is possible first.
