My story

I do not wait
for the right time.

Technology found me early — not through a class or a parent who coded, but through the simple need to know how things worked. Robotics was a regular school activity from Grade 2. By Grade 7 I joined Helios Tech Club and started exploring coding seriously — not because it was required, but because making a computer do something new felt genuinely exciting. By Grade 8 I had built a Discord community for hardware enthusiasts (raided twice, rebuilt three times — now 300+ members). By Grade 10 I was running a battery recycling initiative and speaking about it to a full school auditorium. By Grade 11 I had four internships, a cybersecurity app, a design agency with seven paying clients, and a competition cybersecurity dossier. Nobody assigned any of it.

I sit at the intersection of technology, sustainability, and entrepreneurship — not because it sounds good on paper, but because these are the three things I cannot stop thinking about. Battery Bin is environmental. PhishSentinel is technical. BrandSnap is entrepreneurial. Hardware Decoded is community. CalTrack is AI. My NAS is infrastructure. The thread connecting all of them: I saw something worth building and built it.

Outside the projects: I have played Indian classical flute since Grade 3 — over a decade, uninterrupted, with a school award in Grade 9. I have won gold medals in skating and chess. I taught myself chess seriously during COVID in Grade 7 — Lichess and Chess.com daily for five months, beat my father for the first time, became the chess person of the grade, won two gold medals representing school. I run a personal NAS and maintain a digital library at home because I want to understand infrastructure by actually running it. I have completed Harvard CS50 and The Complete JavaScript Course. I do not start things to have started them.

What drives me

🔍

Curiosity that does not turn off

I ran a phishing analysis before building PhishSentinel. I set up a NAS before I strictly needed one. I joined the AI Club in Grade 9 when AI was barely part of my world — just to understand what was coming. The question always arrives before the project.

🌱

Impact over polish

Battery Bin is a collection bin and a spreadsheet. But 75+ kg of toxic batteries are not in a landfill because of it. I care far more about that number than how any of this looks on paper.

🎵

Long-term commitment

I have played flute for over a decade. I have run Hardware Decoded for years — rebuilt it twice after raids without quitting. The same patience shows up everywhere: instruments, servers, recycling initiatives. Things that last require someone willing to stay.

⚡

Bias toward action

Most of what I have built was not assigned, not required, not expected. It just seemed worth doing. I do not wait for the right conditions or for someone else to prove it is possible first.
